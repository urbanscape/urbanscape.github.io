Born in France, raised in Korea, the Philippines, and France again before moving to the US, Jin is a true citizen of the world (and an avid traveller). He holds a Bachelor's in Architecture from the University of Southern California and a Master's in Urban Planning from Columbia University GSAPP. Currently, he is a Data Engagement Intern at Numina, a computer vision startup, creating meaningful data narratives for clients. Previously, he worked as a designer at JFAK, a boutique architecture firm in Los Angeles, as a research assistant at GSAPP's Housing Lab, and as a co-author on a paper on the State of Privately Owned Public Spaces (POPS) in New York City. Jin's interests include urban data analytics and learning about successful public-private partnerships fostering greater economic development.
