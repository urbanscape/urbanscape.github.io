Born in France, raised in Korea, the Philippines, and France again before moving to the United States, Jin is a true citizen of the world. He holds a Bachelor’s in Architecture from the University of Southern California and a Master’s in Urban Planning from Columbia University GSAPP.
Jin’s career reflects his passion for building--whether that’s paying attention to unseen architectural details that make up a physical space or optimizing algorithms through individual lines of code. His attention to both the macro-vision and micro-precision drives his approach to understanding human-centered transportation systems.
Currently a Data Scientist at Nelson\Nygaard, a transportation consulting firm, Jin leads data initiatives across transit, parking, and transportation demand management projects. His previous experience includes working as a Data Engagement Intern at Numina, a computer vision startup, a designer at JFAK, a boutique architecture firm in Los Angeles, and as a researcher at various research initiatives including New York City’s Privately Owned Public Spaces and Re-coding New York City’s New Law Tenements.
